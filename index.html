About Us

The DSIE (Doctoral Symposium in Informatics Engineering) is part of the "Methodologies for Scientific Research" course held by the Doctoral Program in Informatics Engineering (ProDEI) at Faculty of Engineering of the University of Porto (FEUP).

The next event is the 13th edition of a series of annual scientific meetings which started in 2005. The DSIE'18 (Doctoral Symposium in Informatics Engineering, 2018) will take place at the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Porto (FEUP) between the 25th and 26th January, 2018.

In general lines, the Doctoral Symposium on Informatics Engineering (DSIE’18) aims to improve the PhD students’ proficiency in scientific research, writing and presentation skills as well as to develop their spirit of collaborative research. This Symposium (DSIE’18) provides to doctoral students a platform to present their scientific works, discuss their ideas, collect feedback and profitable expert opinions from peers, professors and experienced researchers. In this event the students will have the opportunity to seek advice on various aspects of performing scientific research in Informatics Engineering, improving their future thesis content and completing a PhD. The sharing of ideas, methods and applications is relevant and will be encouraged, so we invite PhD students to submit their scientific contributions to DSIE’18, in order to share their insights and knowledge on their areas of expertise. This website provides all the information about the DSIE'18 activities. We are delighted to invite you to explore it and get to know about all the details of this event.

Call for Papers

PhD and MsC students working in any area of Informatics Engineering are invited to present their scientific works to discuss their ideas and obtain a relevant feedback and profitable expert opinions from peers, professors and experienced researchers. Authors should submit a paper in English, carefully checked for correct grammar and spelling, addressing one of the conference topics. Each paper should clearly indicate the nature of its technical/scientific contribution, and the problems, domains or environments in which it is applicable. Papers must be formatted according to the Springer LNCS Template , with a length between 10 and 12 pages (including figures and references) and should report on substantial research supported by measurable results.

All proposed papers must be submitted electronically (in PDF format) using the Paper Submission Page (EasyChair system). At least one author for each accepted paper must register to ensure that the paper will be included in the conference proceeding. We would recommend that you respect the page limit and do not submit more than one paper per event. All papers will be single-blind reviewed by the scientific committee members with the aim to provide detailed and constructive feedback. All reviewers recommendations must be addressed by the authors before they submit the final version. Scientific contributions will be evaluated in terms of the following criteria:

- Relevance

- Originality

- Quality (including technical content and accuracy)

- Significance of the work

- Presentation (organization, style and clearness)

- Appropriateness of references to papers presented in earlier editions of the event

Authors of accepted papers will have 15 minutes to present their scientific contribution during the Presentation Sessions, followed by approximately 5 minutes of discussion.

Venue

DSIE’18 will take place in the city of Porto. The scientific program will be held at the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Porto (FEUP). Located in northern Portugal, along the Douro river estuary and bathed by the Atlantic Ocean, Porto is one of the oldest European cities. It was born and developed from the northern bank of this river and its settlement dates back when it was part of the Roman Empire. One of the most significant aspects of Porto is its historical centre and its landscape, combining harmony with the urban structure, presenting a frame of rare beauty. The historical centre, the variety of monumental and artistic patrimony and the Port Wine cellars were some of the reasons why UNESCO classified the city as World Heritage, in 1996. Another of its main icons throughout the world is the famous Port Wine and some of the city highlights include the Port Wine cellars, the Sé Cathedral, a neoclassical Stock Exchange, the Portuguese Manueline-style Church of Santa Clara, the D. Luís I Bridge, the S. Bento Station and Casa da Música. The city is also quite varied, with conservative environment as well as modern living side by side. When discovering Porto, you will also find a contemporary and artistic city, with an intense and welcoming social life which has been growing over the last years. Porto won the European Best Destination 2012, 2014 and 2017 awards and has been experiencing an important touristic and business destination expansion. If you wish to know more information regarding Porto, please have a look at Porto Tourism Guide website.
